Outcome 3 Unit 1


3.1
As highlighted in the other outcomes, the use of VSCode, Teams and Trello were definitely needed or a substitute was needed for this sort of project. The productivity and efficiency of Teams, Slack and Trello is proven which is why it is used so heavily in IT projects and personal projects too. If I had a big team for the website, the trello board would be much more filled out and active. The planning and development side of the project would have had to be looked at as having more effective hours on the project would mean we could be more ambitious and have more people’s input on the final outcome of the project.

3.2
When the people working on the project are happy and passionate towards making the project succeed they are always going to give their best. Taking breaks, bouncing off of other people on the team and just having small discussions towards it all add to team building and the overall drive towards the project. I think great management, open discussion between the team and realistic expectations can make a team very productive and efficient.

3.3
I personally found the trello board for the personal website a double edged sword. It was useful to have everything in one place to look at but it also took time out of the development and testing of the website. Because I was the sole person developing it, I knew what I wanted the website to look and function like, therefore I might not have needed a trello board as much as a team would need it. I also would have liked to have used a different video chatting service as I mentioned, the 40 minute timer on Microsoft Zoom’s service halted productivity for a few minutes every hour when we were on calls. There are free alternatives to this like Google hangouts or the business version of Slack. Slack was very useful for productivity for me as I love to talk to people on the same project for ideas, inspiration and to help each other out. I wouldn’t really need slack for a personal project like a solo website as communication would not be required.

3.4
When testing digital solutions, testing should be carried out at all stages of development. High risk issues should be highlighted first and visible to all the people who need to see it so they can be sorted and fixed before the project moves on. Ignoring issues like these found from testing during development could cause projects to fail in development. Other issues should be analysed and weighed on whether the fix is worth the time and other potential risks from fixing it eg: does it break another function/component in the code. Utilizing Jira for bug tracking and TestRail for running tests are highly efficient and very customizable for any project. Using these however do cost and must be budgeted alongside the other parts of the project.
